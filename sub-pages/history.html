Home History Genres

Video games first started in the early 1950s with simple computer versions of games such as tic tac toe, and checkers. However, it wasn't until the 1970s when video games began to grow in popularity. The company, Atari, designed the first video game to really become a commercial success, Pong. Pong was a coin-operated arcade machine where people would pay a coin, usually a quarter, to play the game for a little while. These arcade games became very popular in the 1980s as a place for kids to spend their free time. Eventually, Pong's success led Atari to design one of the first home video games consoles that are so common today.

In 1985 Nintendo released their first console, the Nintendo Entertainment System or NES. Several other companies eventually released their own home consoles such as SEGA's Genesis, Microsoft's Xbox, and Sony's PlayStation.

Along with home consoles, handheld consoles have also been widely popular. Nintendo's Game Boy, released in 1989, was one of the first handheld consoles to gain popularity. While other handheld consoles have been made, Nintendo has dominated the market ever since those early years.

While some companies, such as Atari and Sega, no longer make consoles, the number of companies and individuals who make and develop video games has grown significantly. Like all technology, videos games have developed over time. While earlier games couldn't store as much information and were a lot simpler to make, video games today are able to store far more information, allowing them to have better design and give the players a more personalized experience.

Although most popular video games are made by bigger companies, there are thousands of games made by smaller studios, or even individuals. These smaller game developers are known as indie developers. Over the years, the world of video games has grown and expanded significantly from where it started in the days of Pong. Now, players are able to choose from thousands of games in a variety of different genres, playstyles, and difficulty levels.
